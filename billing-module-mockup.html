Architecture Note
How Billing Connects to Onboarding
High-level flow for the team
1️⃣
Onboarding Portal
Customer completes form, signs agreement, makes first payment via Razorpay (or offline). Sales confirms → status becomes ops_setup.
2️⃣
Ops Sets Up Account
Ops team provisions the client in c-dash, links their Razorpay customer token, sets plan limits, marks status → active.
3️⃣
c-dash Access Granted
Client gets login credentials via email. Billing tab shows plan from day 1 — first renewal is already scheduled in Razorpay.
4️⃣
Recurring Billing
Razorpay auto-charges on each renewal. Webhook creates a new invoice record, sends email receipt. Failed payments trigger retry + alert.
Phase 1 — Build First
  • Billing Overview screen (plan + next date + amount)
  • Invoice list with PDF download
  • Payment failed alert banner + retry button
  • Saved payment methods list
  • Connect Razorpay webhook → invoice records
Phase 2 — Later
  • Self-serve plan upgrade/downgrade
  • Usage meter alerts (email when 90% hit)
  • Add / change payment method in-app
  • Annual billing option with discount
  • GST invoice with GSTIN auto-fill
Key decision for the team: Recurring payments need Razorpay Subscriptions (auto-mandate) OR a cron job that creates an order + sends payment link each cycle. Subscriptions is simpler and fully automated — customer saves UPI mandate once, gets charged every month. Works for both monthly and quarterly cycles. Sales portal remains the same; ops still confirms account activation.
